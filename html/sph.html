SPH 380 History and Practice of Public Health (5) T. KWAN-GETT, S. PAK-GORSTEIN
Provides a historical overview of public health as a framework to understand organization of contemporary public health and key issues faced today. Offered: AW.
View course details in MyPlan: SPH 380

SPH 381 Science and Public Health (5) M. AVERILL, D. LEHMAN, J. LINGAPPA, G. ONSTAD
Provide an overview and introduction to the ways different scientific disciplines are used to address public health goals. Prerequisite: minimum grade of 1.7 in SPH 380; either BIOL 118, BIOL 180, CHEM 120, CHEM 142, CHEM 145, or both MICROM 301 and MICROM 302. Offered: WSp.
View course details in MyPlan: SPH 381

SPH 480 Research Methods in Public Health (5) J. BASEMAN, L. NELSON
Applies concepts and research methods to real public health problems. Prerequisite: EPI 320; minimum grade of 1.7 in SPH 381. Offered: AW.
View course details in MyPlan: SPH 480

SPH 481 Ethics, Social Justice, and Policy in Public Health (5) S. MACKENZIE
Integration of prior learning and consideration of public health decision from an ethics and justice perspective. Prerequisite: minimum grade of 1.7 in SPH 480. Offered: WSp.
View course details in MyPlan: SPH 481

SPH 490 Selected Topics in Public Health (1-5, max. 15)
In depth study of current public health topics. Offered: AWSpS.
View course details in MyPlan: SPH 490

SPH 491 Public Health Capstone I (2-)
Part one of integrative experience bringing together students' knowledge and skills acquired in the public health major.
View course details in MyPlan: SPH 491

SPH 492 Public Health Capstone II (-3)
Part two of integrative experience bringing together students' knowledge and skills acquired in the public health major. Prerequisite: SPH 491.
View course details in MyPlan: SPH 492

SPH 493 Public Health Capstone (5)
Students complete a final self-directed paper on public health topic of interest integrating the knowledge and skills acquired during their time in the public health major. Offered: AWSpS.

SPH 494 Honors Seminar in Public Health (1, max. 5) D. HINCHEY
Skill-building, discussion-based seminar designed to challenge ability to analyze issues in public health using topics raised in current media. Credit/no-credit only. Offered: AWSp.
View course details in MyPlan: SPH 494

SPH 495 Public Health Internship (1-5, max. 15) D. HINCHEY
Internship in public health related area. Credit/no-credit only. Offered: AWSp.
View course details in MyPlan: SPH 495

SPH 497 Public Health Special Electives (1-5, max. 15)
Independent study of a current public health topic. Offered: AWSpS.
View course details in MyPlan: SPH 497

SPH 499 Undergraduate Research (1-5, max. 15)
Individual research on a specific topic in public health. Offered: AWSpS.
